Cutting a Freehand Brush

 

Use this tool to cut roughly around figures in the design where the rectangular cut cannot be used because of the proximity of other shapes.

To cut a freehand brush, follow these steps:-

  1. Click on the button in the toolbox , or alternatively use the keyboard shortcut CTRL and D.

  2. Zoom in to the area of your design from which you want to cut your brush.

  3. Put Autoscroll on (if you canÂ’t see all of the area that you want to cut on screen).

  1. Select the required background colour . This colour will be transparent when you pick up your brush. If you are cutting out a motif, and want to leave the background behind (for instance, you may want to pick up a floral group from a plain ground), then ensure that the design background colour is selected as your current background colour. You can then draw quite roughly around the shape, rather than going carefully around the outline.

  2. Draw around the shape to be cut, holding down the left-hand mouse button as you draw. When you release the button the start and finish points of your line will be connected and the brush will be created.

  1. If you want to make any colours within the brush transparent , do it at this stage.

  2. The brush will now be displayed in the toolbox. Either place it elsewhere in the design or place it into a shelf box .

 

The brush will remain current until you select another brush from the toolbox or until you cut another brush from the design or select one from the shelf.
