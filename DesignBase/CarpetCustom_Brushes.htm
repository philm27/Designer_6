Custom Brushes

 

It is possible to cut out areas of your design, creating custom brushes. These brushes can then be rotated, mirrored or mitred (if required) and placed elsewhere in the design or saved and imported into other designs.

New brushes will be be initialized to a straight match on being cut from the main design. Brushes can be changed however, by being placed on the shelf, brought in as a design, and their specification can then be edited.

There are five different ways to cut a custom brush  each way being appropriate for different situations. The cutting tools are as follows, with suggestions for their use:

  • Freehand Cut  Use this tool to cut roughly around simple shapes. If you want to cut a brush of a motif that has different top colours to the designs background colours, then this tool is ideal.

  • Polygon Cut  Use this tool to cut around large, detailed shapes. If you need to cut accurately around a shape this is the tool to use!

  • Rectangle Cut  If you want to cut out a sample from a design for developmental or display purposes, then use Rectangular Cut.

  • Elliptical Cut  Use this tool to cut circular or elliptical brushes!

  • Numeric Cut  This facility (menu option Design->Numeric Cut) cuts rectangular brushes numerically (i.e. by specifying the co-ordinates at which to cut it). This can be useful if you are cutting a brush from a large design to ensure that it is cut accurately especially if you are cutting the full width or length of the design.
