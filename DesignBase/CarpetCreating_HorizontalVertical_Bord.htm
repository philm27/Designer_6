Creating Horizontal/Vertical Borders

 

There are many functions to help you design horizontal and vertical borders.

Before starting your border, select a horizontal or vertical repeat type from the specification window .

Having designed one repeating section of your border and you wish to make the design into more than one repeat, then the easiest method is to use duplicate. Alternatively you could use Numeric Paste.

For uneven qualities Â– if you want to turn a horizontal border into a vertical one (or vice versa) then you can rotate it whilst keeping the quality constant.

To create corner sections, try mitering. Mitre the design to give you a mirror image diagonally across the corners making the border run along the adjacent edge.

Use Design->Mirror to mirror in any direction to quickly complete the border. For example if you have completed the bottom left quarter, mirror left to right then bottom to top to complete the other 3 quarters.

Alternatively, many rug designs may involve completing, say, the bottom half then wheeling the bottom half to the top through 180 degrees.

Occasionally you may need to "break" a brush in the middle, bending it to any angle for example to make a section for a hexagon etc.. To do this make a brush representing two sides of the figure then select Brush->Mitre which allows selection of an angle. This function cuts out and discards a triangular segment from the middle of the brush so that it can be folded downwards to a maximum of 90 degrees.
