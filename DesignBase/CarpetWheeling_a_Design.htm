Wheel Design

 

Many Rug designs consist of two halves - one of which is a 180 degree rotation of the other half. This function, called "Design Wheel" allows easy creation of this type of design once one half has been completed.

You can either simply wheel from one half of the design to the other half, or you can double the width or length of the design and simultaneously wheel the original design into the new section.

To access the Wheel function, select menu item Design -> Wheel. The Wheel Options Dialog will be displayed.

Example Wheel Options Dialog showing the bottom to top wheel selected with No Centre Row and not wheeled into doubled up design (i.e. bottom half of current design wheeled into and overwriting existing top half).

Pivot

The Pivot end, or row (pick), divides the design into two halves, one of which is rotated and placed over the top of the other half. The pivot end, or row (pick), itself is not changed by the wheel operation.

If you choose to have "No Centre" then the pivot is an imaginary horizontal or vertical line at the exact centre of the design. These options are only available when the design width (or length) is an even number.

Main Wheel Options

You can choose to wheel from left to right, right to left, bottom to top or top to bottom. Simply click on the appropriate button from the 4 available.

Centering Options Horizontal Wheel

If the design width is an odd number of ends then the following options will be disabled and a simple wheel will be executed with the centre end being the "pivot".

If the design width is an even number then you can choose which, if any, of the two ends in the centre of the design are the "pivot" for the wheel.

If wheeling into doubled up design, only one of the options Left Centre and Right Centre will be available depending on the wheel direction.

No Centre End

This means that the centre of the design will have two identical ends (though one is a 180 degree rotation of the other) as it will have been duplicated. Exactly half the design is wheeled across to the other half.

Left Centre End

In this case the end to the left of the centre line is the pivot for the wheel. Everything to the left or right of it will be wheeled across to the opposite side. This means that the number of ends wheeled across is 1 less than half the design.

For a LR wheel, the extreme right hand end will be unchanged.

For a RL wheel, the extreme right hand end will not be wheeled across.

Right Centre End

In this case the end to the right of the centre line is the pivot for the wheel. Everything to the left or right of it will be wheeled across to the opposite side. As for the previous option the number of ends wheeled across will be 1 less than half the design width.

For a LR wheel, the extreme left hand end will not be wheeled across.

For a RL wheel, the extreme left hand end will be unchanged.

Centering Options Vertical Wheel

If the design length is an odd number of rows(picks) then the following options will be disabled and a simple wheel will be executed with the centre row(pick) being the "pivot".

If wheeling into a doubled up design, only one of the options Above Centre and Below Centre will be available depending on the wheel direction.

No Centre Row

This means that the centre of the design will have two identical rows(picks) (though one is a 180 degree rotation of the other) as it will have been duplicated. Exactly half the design is wheeled up or down to the other half.

Below Centre Row

In this case the row(pick) below the centre line is the pivot line. This row(pick) will be unchanged after the wheel operation. This means that the number of rows(picks) wheeled up or down is 1 less than half the design length.

For a BT wheel, the extreme top row(pick) will be untouched.

For a TB wheel, the extreme top row(pick) will not be wheeled down.

Above Centre Row

In this case the row(pick) above the centre line is the pivot line. This row(pick) will be unchanged after the wheel operation. As in the previous case the number of rows(picks) wheeled up or down will be 1 less than half the design length.

For a BT wheel, the bottom row will not be wheeled up.

For a TB wheel, the bottom row will be untouched.
