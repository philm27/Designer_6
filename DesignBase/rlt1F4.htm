Global Colour Change

 

This function will change all occurences of a particular palette colour to another colour. It will change all motifs of that colour whether connected or not (unlike the normal Fill function).

This technique can be used in 3 different ways:

  • Change an existing design colour to another solid palette colour.

  • Change an existing design colour to a simple moresque pattern made from two separate palette colours.

  • Change an existing design colour to any repeating pattern using the current brush.

 

Solid Colour Global Fill

  1. Select the colour that you want to change TO as either your current foreground or background colour .

  2. Click on the button in the toolbox or, use the keyboard shortcut <Shift> + F. A "paintpot" will appear with your cursor.

  3. Make sure that the current brush is one of the simple solid colour brushes (e.g. 1x1).

  4. Click on the colour in your design (not in the palette) that you want to change FROM. If you click with the left-hand mouse button the colour will change to your current foreground colour. If you click with the right-hand mouse button, the colour will change to your current background colour.

 

Moresque Global Fill

  1. Select your two moresque colours as the current foreground and background colours.

  2. Click on the button in the toolbox or press <Shift> + F on the keyboard. The cursor should change to a "paintpot".

  3. Select a moresque brush from the toolbox

  4. Click with the cursor on the design colour to be changed – the moresque will flood throughout all occurences of that colour whether connected or not.

Note that two moresque brushes are provided - one for each different "footing" i.e. one has the foreground colour at the bottom left, the other has the background colour at bottom left.

 

Custom Brush Global Fill

  1. Select your brush and make sure that the transparency is correct since the brush will be applied with transparency on the right hand mouse button or according to the transparent colour selections if transparency is ON.

  2. Click on the button in the toolbox or press <Shift> + F on the keyboard. The cursor should change to a "paintpot"

  3. Click with the cursor on the design colour to be changed – the custom brush will flood throughout all occurences of that colour whether connected or not.
