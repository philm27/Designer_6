Filling with a Single Color

 

Procedure

To fill an area with color, first make sure the area is completely enclosed otherwise you may get a "leak" from one shape to another of the same colour.

To activate fil, either:-

  • Select the Fill button from the toolbox .

  • Use the keyboard shortcut F

To complete the fill operation simply click with the left (foreground color fill) or right (background color fill) mouse button within the area to be filled.

 

Fill to a Boundary

It is also possible to specify one or more colors to fill to, so that some color boundaries are ignored and filled over.

To achieve this, you simply select your chosen boundary colour as your current background colour (in this instance, the red).

The end result in the above example could be achieved in other ways of course without takign any more time. However, the technique can be useful wherever a multi coloured background needs to be changed to a solid colour (and then, possibly to a different multi coloured effect).
