Normal Working Area

 

The normal working area is specified by the edges of the design window. Within this area you can use any designing tools. Once you go beyond the boundary of the design area the cursor changes from a cross-hair to an arrow - ready for you to make a selection in the toolbox or palette etc.

If you are working in a zoom level that allows you to see an entire single design repeat - when you place a brush over the normal working area boundary, you will see the brush wrap around the design and appear at the opposite edge (its position at this edge will depend on your match type). This allows you to see the effect in the design if you were to actually draw the brush at that location and to adjust the position slightly by moving the mouse before committing to the design.

Note that if the repeat type is Horizontal, wrap around only occurs width wise and in a vertical repeat it only occurs across the top and bottom edges. In the case of a rug, wrap around does not occur in any direction.

If you are working at a high zoom level, and cannot see a full repeat, the part of the brush that goes beyond the normal working area will still be placed on your design even though you cannot see it until you zoom out.
