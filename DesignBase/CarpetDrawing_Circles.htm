Line Thickness

You can use the toolbox or the keyboard to select line thickness:

  • Move the mouse over the "Pen Selector" at the bottom of the toolbox and click on one of the 6 preset pen sizes. Alternatively double click this area to display a dialog permitting a larger size to be set.

  • While in Circle Drawing mode press one of the keys 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 or 6 to select line thickness.

 

Drawing The Circle - Method 1

An arrow will appear on the screen in place of your cursor. To draw your circle:-

  1. Position the arrow at the point which is to become the centre of the circle

  2. Click and hold down the mouse button (left button for foreground colour, right button for background colour).

  3. Continuing to hold the mouse button down, drag the arrow away from that point. The circular outline will increase in size as you drag the arrow further from the centre.

  4. When you reach the desired circle size, let go of the mouse button to secure the size.

Drawing The Circle - Method 2

You can mark the opposite corners of a containing rectangle if you wish for drawing a figure having an even number of ends in the diameter, or an even number of rows in the vertical diameter.

  1. Position the cursor at a point which is to become one corner of the containing rectangle.

  2. Click and hold down the mouse button (left button for foreground colour, right button for background colour).

  3. Hold down the <ctrl> key whilst continuing to hold the mouse button down and drag the arrow to the opposite corner of the containing rectangle.

  4. When you reach the desired point, let go of the mouse button and then the <ctrl> key to secure the size.

Fill to Design Size

The <Home> and <End> keys can be used in a special way to quickly draw a figure that fills the design...

  1. Select the appropriate drawing tool, open or filled circle or ellipse and the line thickness.

  2. Press <Home> key>

  3. Press <End> key

 

If you want to create a solid, single colored shape don't worry about the brush thickness - just make the foreground and background colors the same.

If you want to create a circle with one color for the outline and another for the in-fill, select the line thickness as before to determine outline width.

Create the filled circle using steps 1-4 above.

For a two-colored shape, if you use the left-hand mouse button the outline will be in your foreground colour and the filled circle will be in the background colour. If you use the right-hand mouse button, the opposite will be true.

 

Custom Brush and Filled Circle

It is also possible to create a filled circle made from a custom brush. In this case the line thickness is irrelevant. Cut a brush (or select one from a shelf ) and then create a filled circle as before. You will notice that the circle is made up purely of repeats of your brush. It will not have a different outline colour.

Note that the Brush Handle is used as follows:

If the Handle is Centre then the centre of the brush will be in the centre of the circle.

If the Handle is at one of the four corners, then that corner of the brush will be positioned at the same corner of the imaginary rectangle enclosing the circle and the brush will be repeated outwards from that point.

The current transparency setting will be used.
