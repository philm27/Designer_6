Using the Information Panel

 

The Information Panel is a small window that follows the cursor about the screen  giving you information about the position of the cursor.

Find the Information Panel in the VIEW menu, or alternatively use the keyboard shortcut <F11>. To turn the panel off, just de-select it from the drop-down menu or press the <F11> key again.

As you float the cursor over your design the information panel tells you its x and y coordinate. It also tells you the distance between the bottom left-hand corner of the design and the cursor in the horizontal and vertical axis. (If the specification window has been set up using a metric scale, then the information will be shown in centimetres. If it has been set up using an imperial scale, then the information will be given in inches.)

Depending on what function you are working in, the information panel will display different data. Here are some examples:-

(A)

Example of type (A) information panel showing that the cursor is over end 106 row (pick) 106 which is 13.25 inches and 12.62 inches from the left edge and bottom edge respectively. (Note that measurements will be shown in cm if the quality (setting) is expressed as ends per cm or per 10 cm in the specification).

(B)

Example of type (B) information panel whilst the mouse button is depressed during a line drawing operation showing that the line started at end 106 row (pick) 106 and is now at end 129 row (pick) 147. The shape drawn has a width of 3.00 inches and a length of 5.00 inches which is 24 ends and 42 rows (picks). The line is at an angle of 30.5 degrees to the vertical.

 

  • Line Draw  Whilst the mouse is hovering over the design, before the mouse button is pressed, the type (A) information panel is shown displaying the end and row(pick) of the grid point under the cursor. After the button has been pressed but not released, the panel changes to type (B) as shown above which shows the start point, the end point, the same thing in inches and the angle of the line. After the button has been released to fix the line the display reverts back to type (A) ready for another line to be drawn.

  • Square /Rectangle Draw and Rectangular Cut  The panel displays the start and finish co-ordinates of the square/rectangle, and its width and length.

  • Circle /Ellipse Draw and Circular Cut  The panel gives the centre location of the shape and its radius measurements in the horizontal and vertical axis.

  • Duplicate  The panel gives the start and finish co-ordinates of the duplicating section, and also its dimensions in the horizontal and vertical axis.
