Polygons - Introduction

Polygons in Designer programs is a general term for a set of drawing objects that may in fact consist of:

  • Straight Lines

  • Curves

  • Circles

  • Ellipses

  • Arcs

  • Chords

Summary of Main Features

  • These figures are drawn extremely precisely to a thousandth of a millimetre and are not tied to the quality of the design.

  • Several Lines and Curves may join together to form a closed area or they may simply form a line.

  • Several individual Straight Lines may be mixed with one or more curves to form a longer line or closed shape.

  • Polygons can be grouped together to any number of levels and given names to assist with their management.

  • Groups or individual polygons can be locked in place to prevent accidental movement.

  • Every Polygon has properties such as the line thickness, line colour and fill colour so that it knows how to draw itself, if and when required.

 

Benefits of Using Polygons

  • Making a motif using polygons means that it is incredibly easy to copy, re-size, rotate, flip etc, all without losing any detail from the motif.

  • New lines can be created that are parallel to an existing figure, either inside or outside closed figures, or, to the left or right of curves and lines not forming part of a closed figure.

  • A change of quality again results in a perfect image with no loss of detail in the polygon figures.

  • One or several polygons can be designated as Guide Lines in any appropriate colour. Guide Lines can be switched on or off whilst working and they may represent the location of motifs or possibly the location of special features in a custom carpet such as a bar area or stairwell.

  • A library of polygon figures can be built over time - all applicable to any quality.

 

Creating Polygons

Polygons can be created by two main methods, plus an option while using Tape Measure...

  • Use "New Polygon" function in Polygon Mode to draw them

  • Use "New Standard Shape" to generate what might be a complex shape automatically, possibly with borders

  • Simple Straight Guide Lines can be created as a bi-product of using the Tape Measure Tool*

* Note that these lines are added to the current set of Polygons but are automatically flagged as Guide Lines. (To see Guide Lines whilst designing select the menu option View->GuideLines).
