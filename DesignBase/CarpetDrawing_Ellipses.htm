Drawing Ellipses

 

Use the same principles for drawing ellipses as for drawing circles . The only difference is that when you drag the arrow away from the centre of the ellipse you are nominating the position of the corner of a rectangle containing the ellipse.

There are two options  you can choose either to draw an outline, or to draw a filled ellipse.

 

Ellipse

To draw an elliptical outline, either:-

  • Click on the button in the toolbox

  • Use the keyboard shortcut C

 

Line Thickness

You can use the toolbox or the keyboard to select line thickness:

  • Move the mouse over the "Pen Selector" at the bottom of the toolbox and click on one of the 6 preset pen sizes. Alternatively double click this area to display a dialog permitting a larger size to be set.

  • While in Circle Drawing mode press one of the keys 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 or 6 to select line thickness.

 

Drawing The Ellipse

An arrow will appear on the screen in place of your cursor. To draw your ellipse:-

  1. Position the arrow at the point which is to become the centre of the ellipse

  2. Click and hold down the mouse button (left button for foreground colour, right button for background colour).

  3. Continuing to hold the mouse button down, drag the arrow away from the ellipses centre. The outline will increase in size as you drag the arrow further from the centre.

  4. By moving the mouse left or right you increase or decrease the horizontal radius.

  5. By moving the mouse up or down you increase or decrease the vertical radius.

  6. When you reach the desired shape and size, let go of the mouse button to fix it.

 

Filled Ellipse

Either:

  • Click on the button in the toolbox

  • Use the keyboard shortcut <shift>+C

 

If you want to create a solid, single coloured shape dont worry about brush thicknesses - just make the foreground and background colours the same.

If you want to create an ellipse with one colour for the outline and another for the infill, then select your brush thickness as before to determine outline width.

Create the filled ellipse using steps 1  6 above.

For a two-coloured shape  if you use the left-hand mouse button the outline will be in your foreground colour and the filled ellipse will be in the background colour. If you use the right-hand mouse button, the opposite will be true.

 

Custom Brush and Filled Ellipse

It is also possible to create a filled ellipse made from a custom brush. In this case the line thickness is irrelevant. Cut a brush (or select one from a shelf ) and then create a filled ellipse as before. You will notice that the shape is made up purely of repeats of your brush  it doesnt have a different outline colour.

Note that the Brush Handle is used as follows:

If the Handle is Centre then the centre of the brush will be in the centre of the ellipse.

If the Handle is at one of the four corners than that corner of the brush will be positioned at the same corner of the imaginary rectangle enclosing the ellipse and the brush will be repeated outwards from that point.

The current transparency setting will be used.
