Scrolling around the Design

 

There are a number of different techniques for "scrolling" or moving the design around the screen.

 

Move by Hand

Click on the move button in the toolbox, or use the keyboard shortcut M. The cursor will change to a hand pointer and you will find that you can "push" the design around on the screen using the mouse. Simply press the mouse button and drag the design around. Release the mouse button to stop. You can repeat this action as many times as you wish.

When you want to begin using a drawing function again simply select the required button in the toolbox.

Note that you can scroll right across the edges of a normal repeat whereas a Rug will not scroll past the edges. Similarly a Horizontal repeat will not scroll past its top and bottom edges and a Vertical repeat will not scroll past its left and right edges.

 

Keyboard

Use the cursor keys to scroll around in any direction. Use the up arrow to move towards the top of the design. Down arrow to move towards the bottom of the design. Left arrow to move towards the left of the design. Right arrow to move towards the right edge of the design. Each time you press a key, the design will move by one end or row (pick).

You can also hold a cursor key down to keep the design scrolling until you release the key.

You can also press <Shift> and a cursor key to move half a screen full at a time.

You can also press <Ctrl> and a cursor key to move to the edge of the design.

For a full list of Keyboard shortcuts see the topic Keyboard Shortcuts.

 

Numeric Keypad

The keys on the numeric keypad can be used to move to the corners, edges or centre of the design as follows. If you think of the keypad as representing the design, then each key will take you to the corresponding area of the design. For example the 7 key is normally at the top left of the keypad and it will take you to the top left of the design.

7 Top Left

8 Top Edge

9 Top Right

4 Left Edge

5 Centre

6 Right edge

1 Bottom Left

2 Bottom Edge

3 Bottom Right

0 Bottom Left corner to centre*

. (dot) Bottom Right corner to centre*

* Note that the 0 and .(dot) options move the corners of the design to the centre of the screen whereas the other options move the appropriate corner (or edge) to about one sixth of the way across the screen. For a Rug, the design will not normally be shown in repeat.

 

Autoscroll

When this option is selected your design will automatically scroll when you hit the edge of the working area when using certain drawing functions.

For example, if you are drawing a line using the line drawing function, then lick and drag the mouse to a point off the viewing area, then the design will automatically scroll so that you can select the end point of the line.

The design will not scroll unless the drawing function requires the use of "click and drag".

Some examples of functions that will use Autoscroll if selected are: Line drawing, Rectangle, Circle, Ellipse, Spray, Rectangular Cut, Tape Measure.

To select Autoscroll:-

  • Select menu option View->Autoscroll, or

  • Click on the Autoscroll button in the toolbar, or

  • Use the keyboard shortcut A
