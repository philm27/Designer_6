Merge View

A view option in the Weave Editor that allows all the threads in an artwork row to be shown on the same row in the view. Also, the user can be selective about which colors (or levels) are actually shown at any one time. The aspect ratio will thus be approximately correct for the artwork. If two or more colors are lifted or dropped at the same time, the colors are merged.

At present this is only available for designs that use weft figuring or are 1:2 or 1:3 warp to weft ratio.

You can switch between Normal View and Merge View using the additional tool bar at the bottom of the information panel as shown here.

An example of the Information Panel whilst using Merge View. The Selectors Tool bar allows the user to switch to or from Normal Mode and Merge Mode and also allows Selectors to be switched on or off . In this example the user is in Merge Mode and selectors 3 and 4 (the right hand two color buttons) are selected and displayed.

 

Example of a small section of Merge View. Artwork rows 1-3 consist of a single level, rows 4-8 have 2 levels and rows 9-10 have 3 levels. Three different colors are shown in the face. The bottom left being the background blue with a motif in grey and beige at the top right. In this case the grey and beige are always crammed (shown by the + sign). The colored squares in the weft bar represent the weft threading plan with the first of each group of picks being on the left. The pick number on the right of the weft bar is the first of those picks (for example row 10 has 3 picks 17-19)

In this example the grey and beige have been switched off using the buttons at the bottom of the information panel. A cross is drawn through these colors in the weft bar on the left. The weave itself shows only the blue in selector 1. Even though the grey and beige are not shown in the weave, the cramming mark can still be edited in the usual way.

The same section of the design showing the artwork only.

 

 

You can edit the colors and cramming in the normal way. Obviously this will automatically be reflected in the threading plan.

Note, however,  that you cannot display nor modify the weft controls A-Z whilst using Merge View.
