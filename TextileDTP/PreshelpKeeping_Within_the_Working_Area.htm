Keeping Within the Working Area

 

When you set out your layout, you view the complete page(s). However, when you print the layout, only objects that lie within the page margins will print. As a safeguard, you can request that no objects go beyond the page margins  this feature is called Clipping to Page.

To activate this feature, select CLIP TO PAGE from the VIEW drop-down menu. Once selected, you will see a next to the words Clip to Page in the menu. To deselect this option, simply click on it again to remove the .

 

Having selected Clip to Page, it is still possible to insert objects outside of the page(s) (see Understanding Working Boundaries ), but once an object has been brought onto the page you cannot drag it outside the page margins. Everyone develops their own working methods, but it is a good idea to leave Clip to Page deselected until you get to the fine tuning of a layout  this leaves you free to move objects anywhere within the screen at the initial stages, and gives you the assurance that your layout will print out in its entirity when complete.
