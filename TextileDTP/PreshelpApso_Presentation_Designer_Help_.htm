Textile Desktop Publisher Help

 

Welcome to the Help Files! They have been written to help you, the designer, get the most out of APSO Textile Desktop Publishing package. They are intended for use by both new and experienced customers as there may well be facilities that even the most experienced APSO users have never had a chance to explore.

 

Everything is explained in easy stages and in everyday language. The Desktop Publishing software has been designed to be user-friendly - you do not need to be a computer wizard to use it!

 

How to use the Help Files

 

To get a general picture of the facilities available to you as a designer, browse through the CONTENTS LIBRARY, and read topics that are of interest. To get to the Contents Library from this page, click on Contents at the top of the page.

 

To get help on a specific topic, select the topic from the INDEX. To get to the Index from this page, click on Index at the top of the page.

 

If a Help topic is fairly long, it will have a sliding bar on its right-hand edge. By moving the sliding bar you can scroll down the page to read the whole topic.

 

If a topic contains a large illustration, it will have a sliding bar across the bottom of the window. To view the illustration in its entirety either scroll across the page using the sliding bar, or maximise the window.

 

When you come across words highlighted in purple, click on the highlighted lettering to jump to another related topic.

 

If a Help topic has a "See Also" button at the end, it means that there are related topics that may be of interest to you, but that have not been mentioned in the current topic. To access these topics, click on the button and then click on the topics highlighted in purple.

 

Many of the graphics within the Help File hold hidden information Â– by clicking on areas within the graphics, this information will be disclosed.

 

When explaining how to use Textile DTP, the Help Files always refer to the left and right-hand buttons on the mouse. If you are working with a pen and digitiser, then for left-hand mouse button read nib of pen and for right-hand mouse button read side button on pen.

 

Having displayed a Help topic, you can move to another topic using the buttons at the top of the Help topic window in three ways:-

  1. Click on CONTENTS to return to the Contents Library.

  2. Click on INDEX to return to the Index.

  3. Click on BACK to return to the Help topic that you had displayed before the present one.

It is also possible to print a topic by simply clicking on PRINT.

  • Having read a Help topic, you can minimise it to the task bar when you return to your presentation work - so that it is readily available to bring back up on screen if you want to refer to it.
