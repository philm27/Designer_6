Selecting, Grouping and Locking Objects

 

To select a single object, click on the icon in the toolbox and then click on the object. Once selected, the object is given Handles so that it can be resized .

You must always select a design before trying to insert any of its associated objects.

If you want to select an object that is placed within another object, you select it in the following way:-

 

  1. Select the icon from the toolbox.

  2. Click and drag a rectangle, starting from the background page, to encompass the whole of the required object without encompassing all four corners of the unwanted object:-

To select all of the objects within the working area, select SELECT ALL from the EDIT drop-down menu .

To select a group of objects so that they can be moved and scaled together, use either of the following procedures:-

 

  1. Select the icon from the toolbox.

  2. Click and drag a rectangle to encompass all of the objects that you want to group together

  3. Click on the icon on the toolbar .

 

OR

  1. Select the icon from the toolbox

  2. Select the objects that you want to group together by clicking on them whilst holding down the <CTRL> key on the keyboard

  3. Click on the icon on the toolar.

 

You will notice that the handles are now at the corners of the group, rather than the corners of the individual objects. The objects will remain Grouped until you click on the icon to Ungroup them.

 

If you want to ensure that an object or group of objects isnÂ’t accidentally moved or resized within the layout, you can lock the object/group. Simply select the object or group and then click on the icon on the toolbar. A padlock will now appear at the bottom right-hand corner of the object/group to indicate that it is locked and cannot be moved or resized. To release the padlock, click on the icon on the toolbar.
